A Data Analyst turns raw data into insights that guide decisions. In practice, this means gathering data, cleaning it, analyzing patterns, and communicating results clearly to non-technical stakeholders.

Core skills

  • SQL: query databases, join tables, aggregate metrics.
  • EDA: understand distributions, missing values, and outliers.
  • Visualization: charts that highlight trends and comparisons.
  • Storytelling: concise summaries, KPIs, and actionable recommendations.

Simple learning plan

  1. Master SQL SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, JOIN, and window functions.
  2. Use Python (pandas) or spreadsheets for cleaning and quick EDA.
  3. Practice building a one-page dashboard (e.g., monthly sales KPIs).
  4. Publish your findings (like this blog!) and collect feedback.

Takeaway

Consistency beats intensity. Ship small analyses often, ask questions, and refine your workflows.
